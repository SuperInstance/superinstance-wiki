## Vector A: CRDT → Constraint Theory → FLUX Polyglot

**Origin:** SmartCRDT (Dec 2025) — "distributed consensus for self-improving AI"
**Evolution:**
1. Apr 2026: Forgemaster born — "constraint theory migration specialist"
2. May 5: constraint-theory-ecosystem — "tolerance stacks, interference fits, o-rings"
3. May 13: constraint-theory-py — Python library
4. May 14: tensor-spline — Eisenstein lattice splines for neural compression
5. May 19: flux-* explosion — 15 languages for constraint checking

**Headspace shift:**
- Dec 2025: "How do agents agree on state?" (CRDT)
- Apr 2026: "How do we verify exact geometric constraints?" (Forgemaster)
- May 2026: "How do we make every language speak constraints?" (FLUX)

**The through-line:** The desire for *exactness* and *distributed agreement* never changed. What changed was the expression: from CRDTs (data structures) to constraint theory (mathematics) to FLUX (universal language).
